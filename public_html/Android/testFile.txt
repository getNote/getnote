(a) Except as authorized by policies developed in accordance\r\nwith subdivision (a) of Section 66452, no business, agency, or\r\nperson, including, but not necessarily limited to, an enrolled\r\nstudent, shall prepare, cause to be prepared, give, sell, transfer,\r\nor otherwise distribute or publish, for any commercial purpose, any\r\ncontemporaneous recording of an academic presentation in a classroom\r\nor equivalent site of instruction by an instructor of record. This\r\nprohibition applies to a recording made in any medium, including, but\r\nnot necessarily limited to, handwritten or typewritten class notes.\r\n   (b) Nothing in this section shall be construed to interfere with\r\nthe rights of disabled students under law.\r\n   (c) As used in this section:\r\n   (1) \"Academic presentation\" means any lecture, speech,\r\nperformance, exhibit, or other form of academic or aesthetic\r\npresentation, made by an instructor of record as part of an\r\nauthorized course of instruction that is not fixed in a tangible\r\nmedium of expression.\r\n   (2) \"Commercial purpose\" means any purpose that has financial or\r\neconomic gain as an objective.\r\n   (3) \"Instructor of record\" means any teacher or staff member\r\nemployed to teach courses and authorize credit for the successful\r\ncompletion of courses.\r\n\r\n66451.  (a) Any court of competent jurisdiction may grant relief\r\nthat it finds necessary to enforce this chapter, including the\r\nissuance of an injunction. Any person injured by a violation of this\r\nchapter, in addition to actual damages, may recover court costs,\r\nattorney's fees, and a civil penalty from any person who is not a\r\nstudent enrolled in the institution at which the instructor of record\r\nmakes his or her academic presentation and who seeks to obtain\r\nfinancial or economic gain through the unauthorized dissemination of\r\nthe academic presentation. The amount of the civil penalty shall not\r\nexceed one thousand dollars ($1,000) for the first offense, five\r\nthousand dollars ($5,000) for the second offense, and for any\r\nsubsequent offense, a penalty of not less than ten thousand dollars\r\n($10,000) or more than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000).\r\n   (b) Actions for any relief pursuant to this chapter may be\r\nprosecuted in a court of competent jurisdiction by the Attorney\r\nGeneral or any district attorney or by any county counsel authorized\r\nby agreement with the district attorney in actions involving\r\nviolation of a county ordinance, or any city attorney of a city, or\r\ncity and county, having a population in excess of 750,000, and, with\r\nthe consent of the district attorney, by a city prosecutor in any\r\ncity having a full-time city prosecutor or, with the consent of the\r\ndistrict attorney, by a city attorney in any city, or city and\r\ncounty, in the name of the people of the State of California upon\r\ntheir own complaint or upon the complaint of any board, officer,\r\nperson, corporation, or association or by any person acting for the\r\ninterests of itself, its members, or the general public.\r\n   (c) It does not constitute a violation of this chapter for a\r\nbusiness, agency, or person solely to provide access or connection to\r\nor from a facility, system, or network over which that business,\r\nagency, or person has no control, including related capabilities that\r\nare incidental to providing access or connection. This subdivision\r\ndoes not apply to a business or agency that is owned by, or to a\r\nbusiness, agency, or person that is controlled by, or a conspirator\r\nwith, a business, agency, or person actively involved in the\r\ncreation, editing, or knowing distribution of a contemporaneous\r\nrecording that violates this chapter.\r\n\r\n66452.  (a) The Regents of the University of California and the\r\ngoverning boards of private postsecondary institutions are requested\r\nto, the Trustees of the California State University shall, and the\r\ngoverning board of each community college district may, in\r\nconsultation with faculty, in accordance with applicable procedures,\r\ndevelop policies to prohibit the unauthorized recording,\r\ndissemination, and publication of academic presentations for\r\ncommercial purposes. Nothing in this chapter is intended to change\r\nexisting law as it pertains to the ownership of academic\r\npresentations.\r\n   (b) The Regents of the University of California and the governing\r\nboards of private postsecondary institutions are requested to, the\r\nTrustees of the California State University shall, and the governing\r\nboard of each community college district may, adopt or provide for\r\nthe adoption of specific regulations governing a violation of this\r\nchapter by students, along with applicable penalties for a violation\r\nof the regulations.
