<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Information Technology Security
Evaluation Criteria (ITSEC)</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Drafted by European countries in
	1990</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Includes concepts from TCSEC
	(Orange book)</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Thought orange book was too rigid</p>
	</li></ol>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Adds integrity and availability as
	security goals</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Recognizes that the security of an
	IT system can often be achieved through non technical measures, such
	as organizational and administrative controls</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Ratings based on effectiveness and
	correctness</p></li></ol>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">International Computer Security
Association (ICSA)</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Goal is to develop criteria for at
	least 80% of commercial off the shelf (COTS) products</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Criteria are based on threats that
	occur most frequently</p></li></ol>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Certification and Accreditation</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Set of procedures and judgments
	assessing the suitability of a system to operate in a target
	operational environment</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Often used for non certified
	products</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Certification</p>
		<ol>
			<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Endorsement that the system
			application meets their functional and security requirements</p>
		</li></ol>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Accreditation</p>
		<ol>
			<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Formal approval by management</p></li></ol></li></ol></li></ol>

against threats to:</p>
<ol>
