<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Three types of Attacks</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Attacks that appeal to the vanity
	or ego of the victim</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">pick victims that feel
		under-appreciated and/or in a position that is beneath their
		talents</p>
	</li></ol>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">attacks that take advantage of the
	feeling of sympathy or empathy</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">fellow employee / contractor /
		vendor who is in a “real jam” and needs assistance to get some
		tasks done immediately</p>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">cannot remember user-name or
		password, or locked himself out</p>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">urgency used to circumvent the
		normal, required procedures</p>
	</li></ol>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Loyalty is gained through hard
	work and perseverance, but gained faster through intimidation</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">attacks that are based on
		intimidation</p>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">pretends to be an authority
		figure</p>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">if pretending to be from law
		enforcement then the scenario usually involves some “hush-hush”
		investigation or national security issue and employee must not tell

<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Layering</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Lower layers provide primitive
	functions while the higher layers use the primitive functions to
	provide more complex functions</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">examples</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">first layer OS</p>
		</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Applications run on top of OS</p></li></ol></li></ol>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Security &amp; Layering</p>
<ol>
	<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Constrains functions within each
	layer, to that layer</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">Can't just have a single security
	layer</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">each layer has its own set of
		objects requiring secure management</p>
	</li></ol>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">each layer should provide security
	features</p>
	</li><li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">security primitives should be
	placed in the lowest level possible</p>
	<ol>
		<li><p style="margin-bottom: 0in">if you gained access to the so
		level then you could change security measures in the application
		level</p></li></ol></li></ol>
